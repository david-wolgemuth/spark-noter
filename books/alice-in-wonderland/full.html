Chapter II: The Pool of Tears

Grade 5

In Chapter II, Alice finds herself growing larger and smaller, causing her to feel disoriented. She cries a pool of tears and becomes trapped in it. As she swims in the pool, she encounters a mouse and tries to engage in conversation, but the mouse is offended by the mention of cats. Eventually, Alice and the other creatures in the pool make their way to the shore.

Chapter III: A Caucus-Race and a Long Tale

Grade 5

In Chapter III, Alice finds herself in the company of a group of wet and uncomfortable animals and birds. They discuss how to get dry and eventually decide to have a Caucus-race. The race is chaotic, with no clear start or end, but eventually, they all become dry again. The Dodo suggests that everyone has won and should receive prizes. Alice hands out comfits as prizes, and the Dodo presents her with a thimble. After eating the comfits, the group asks the Mouse to tell them a story. The Mouse begins a sad tale, but Alice gets distracted and doesn't pay attention. The Mouse becomes angry and walks away, leaving Alice and the others disappointed. The chapter ends with Alice longing for her cat Dinah and hoping that the Mouse will return to finish the story.

Chapter IV: The Rabbit Sends in a Little Bill

Grade 5

In this chapter, Alice encounters the White Rabbit again, who is anxiously searching for something. The Rabbit mistakes Alice for his housemaid and orders her to fetch his gloves and fan. Alice, frightened, runs off to find the items. She stumbles upon a house with a brass plate engraved with 'W. RABBIT' and enters to search for the fan and gloves. Inside, she discovers a bottle and drinks from it, hoping it will make her grow larger. However, she ends up growing too large and becomes trapped in the house. Eventually, she eats a cake that makes her shrink back to her normal size and escapes the house. Outside, she encounters a crowd of animals and birds, including a puppy. After playing with the puppy, Alice continues her journey to find her way into the lovely garden. She then comes across a large mushroom and spots a caterpillar smoking a hookah on top.

Chapter V: Advice from a Caterpillar

Grade 5

In Chapter V, Alice encounters a Caterpillar and engages in a conversation with it. The Caterpillar questions Alice about her identity and the changes she has undergone. Alice expresses her confusion and frustration at her changing size and the Caterpillar dismisses her concerns. They discuss the concept of transformation and the Caterpillar offers cryptic advice. Alice tries to understand the meaning of the Caterpillar's words but is left puzzled. The Caterpillar eventually leaves, mentioning the effects of a mushroom on one's size. Alice experiments with the mushroom and experiences drastic changes in her height. She encounters a Pigeon that mistakes her for a serpent and they argue about her true nature. Eventually, Alice returns to her normal size and continues her journey towards a beautiful garden.

Chapter VI: Pig and Pepper

Grade 6

In Chapter VI, titled 'Pig and Pepper,' Alice encounters a peculiar house and two footmen in livery. The footmen deliver an invitation from the Queen to play croquet to the Duchess. Alice observes their unusual behavior and decides to listen in. She witnesses a chaotic scene inside the house, with the Duchess nursing a baby and the cook stirring a cauldron of soup. The kitchen is filled with smoke and pepper, causing Alice to sneeze. The Duchess's baby sneezes and howls incessantly, while the cook throws objects around the room. Alice tries to intervene but is met with indifference. Eventually, the Duchess leaves to play croquet, leaving Alice to care for the baby, which she realizes has turned into a pig. Alice then encounters the Cheshire Cat, who informs her about the mad inhabitants of the area and disappears and reappears mysteriously. Alice decides to continue her journey to find the March Hare's house, hoping for a less mad encounter.

Chapter VII: A Mad Tea-Party

Grade 6

In 'A Mad Tea-Party,' Alice stumbles upon a table set under a tree where the March Hare and the Hatter are having tea. The Dormouse is asleep and used as a cushion by the other two. Alice joins the tea party despite being told there's no room. They offer her non-existent wine, leading to a series of nonsensical conversations. The Hatter asks Alice a riddle about a raven and a writing-desk, which he doesn't know the answer to. They discuss the concept of Time and its peculiarities. The Hatter and the March Hare explain that it's always tea-time for them due to a quarrel with Time. The Dormouse tells a disjointed story about three sisters living in a treacle-well, drawing treacle and other things that begin with the letter 'M'. The chaotic and confusing tea party ends with Alice leaving in frustration and finding herself back in the long hall, ready to explore the beautiful garden.

Chapter VIII: The Queen’s Croquet-Ground

Grade 6

In Chapter VIII, Alice finds herself in the Queen's Croquet-Ground. She observes three gardeners painting white roses red, fearing the Queen's wrath if she discovers the mistake. Alice questions the gardeners, but they remain silent. The Queen arrives with her entourage, including soldiers, courtiers, and the royal children. The game of croquet begins, with live hedgehogs as balls and flamingoes as mallets. The chaotic game leads to the Queen's frequent outbursts and threats of beheading. Alice encounters the Cheshire Cat, who warns her about the Duchess's impending execution. As Alice tries to navigate the game, she witnesses a dispute between the executioner, the King, and the Queen over the beheading of a headless creature. Alice suggests asking the Duchess, who is in prison. The chapter ends with the search for the Cat's head and the resumption of the game.

Chapter IX: The Mock Turtle’s Story

Grade 6

In Chapter IX, Alice encounters the Duchess again, who is in a pleasant temper. They discuss the effects of pepper on one's temperament and the importance of morals. The Queen interrupts their conversation and threatens to execute Alice or her head. The Duchess chooses to leave, and Alice continues playing croquet with the Queen. The Queen pardons all the other players and takes Alice to meet the Mock Turtle. The Gryphon explains that the Mock Turtle's sorrow is only imaginary. The Mock Turtle begins to tell his history, revealing that he was once a real turtle and attended school in the sea. He describes the subjects he learned, including Reeling, Writhing, Arithmetic, and Mystery. The Gryphon adds that he learned Classics from an old crab. The Mock Turtle and Gryphon become emotional when discussing their lessons in Laughing and Grief. Alice asks about their daily lesson schedule, and they explain that the lessons lessen each day. The Gryphon changes the subject to games, ending the chapter.

Chapter X: The Lobster Quadrille

Grade 5

In Chapter X, titled 'The Lobster Quadrille,' Alice encounters the Mock Turtle and the Gryphon. The Mock Turtle, overcome with emotion, explains the delight of a Lobster Quadrille dance. The Gryphon adds details about the dance formation and the role of lobsters as partners. The creatures excitedly demonstrate the dance, singing a song about the whiting. Alice expresses her interest in the dance and the song, and the Mock Turtle and Gryphon share more information about whiting and its uses. They also discuss the peculiar lyrics of the song. Alice then recounts her adventures to the curious creatures, who eagerly listen. The chapter ends with the Gryphon suggesting another dance figure or a song by the Mock Turtle, but they are interrupted by the start of a trial, prompting them to rush off.

Chapter XI: Who Stole the Tarts?

Grade 6

In Chapter XI, Alice finds herself in a court of justice where the King and Queen of Hearts are presiding over a trial. The Knave of Hearts is standing before them in chains, accused of stealing tarts. Alice observes the court proceedings and recognizes the judge, jury, and other elements of a trial. The Hatter is called as the first witness and gives a confusing testimony about tea and bread-and-butter. The Dormouse and the March Hare also contribute to the chaotic proceedings. Alice grows larger during the trial and experiences discomfort. The cook is called as the next witness but refuses to give evidence. The court descends into chaos, and the cook disappears. The King and Queen express relief and call Alice as the next witness, surprising her.

Chapter XII: Alice’s Evidence

Grade 6

In Chapter XII, Alice jumps up in a hurry and accidentally tips over the jury-box, causing the jurymen to fall on the heads of the crowd. She quickly picks them up, reminiscent of a previous incident with goldfish. The King insists that the trial cannot proceed until all the jurymen are back in their proper places. Alice realizes that she had put the Lizard in the jury-box upside down and corrects it. The jury then diligently writes out a history of the accident. The King questions Alice about her knowledge of the business, to which she responds that she knows nothing. The White Rabbit interrupts with a letter that has just been picked up, but it turns out to be a set of verses instead. The King and the jury try to decipher the meaning of the verses, while Alice expresses her disbelief in their significance. The King orders the jury to consider their verdict, but the Queen insists on having the sentence first. Alice defies the Queen, declaring that they are nothing but a pack of cards. The pack of cards rises up and flies at Alice, causing her to wake up from her dream. Alice tells her sister about her strange adventures, and her sister reflects on the wonderful dream and imagines Alice sharing her adventures with her own children in the future.
