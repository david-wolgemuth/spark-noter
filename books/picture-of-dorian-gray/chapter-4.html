Chapter V

Grade 8

In Chapter V, Sibyl Vane expresses her happiness to her mother, Mrs. Vane, about her love for a man named Prince Charming. Mrs. Vane, however, is more concerned about their debts and the money they owe to Mr. Isaacs. Sibyl dismisses the importance of money and declares her love for Prince Charming. Mrs. Vane warns Sibyl about the consequences of their financial situation and the need to consider a wealthy suitor. Sibyl remains infatuated with Prince Charming and believes that love is more important than money. Jim Vane, Sibyl's brother, expresses his distrust and jealousy towards Prince Charming. Sibyl defends her love for him and insists that she will love him forever. The chapter ends with Jim vowing to protect Sibyl and threatening to harm Prince Charming if he ever hurts her.
